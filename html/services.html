Physical Therapy


We can help minimize your back and neck pain with a customized treatment plan based on:


  • Stretching and strengthening,
  • Proper posture and ergonomic principles to preserve the spine.
  • Apply passive modalities if needed: ice, heat, ultrasound, traction, electrical stimulation.
  • Education, to decrease the likelihood of future injuries.

When life throws you a curve ball, rest assured that we’ll be here with the care you need, when you need it.

Rheumatology


Rheumatology is a subspecialty of internal medicine that is devoted to the diagnosis and treatment of rheumatic diseases. Rheumatologists mainly deal with clinical problems involving joints, soft tissues, and related conditions of connective tissues. We are committed to helping patients with rheumatoid arthritis and related conditions.

Joint Replacement


We perform arthroplasty (joint replacement) for the shoulder, hip, knee, and ankle using the latest, most advanced surgical techniques. When only some of the joint is damaged, we repair or replace just the damaged parts. When the entire joint is damaged, a total joint replacement is performed. To replace a hip or knee joint, we removes the diseased or damaged parts and inserts artificial parts, called prostheses or implants.

Osteoporosis Care


Osteoporosis is a medical condition in which the bones become extremely porous, brittle, and fragile due to loss of tissue caused by hormonal changes, calcium deficiency, or vitamin D deficiency. Bones can become weak and may break easily. In the spine, osteoporosis is the most common cause of vertebral compression fractures.

Sports Medicine


For an athlete, injuries mean downtime. Some of the most common sports injuries affect the shoulder, elbow, and knee. The orthopedic and sports medicine specialists can explain the nature of your injury, options that are available to you, and recommend a course of action that is best for your situation.

Trauma


Trauma is any injury that occurs during physical activity, motor vehicle accidents, electric shock, or other activities. A bone may become fractured completely or partially and is often caused from trauma due to a fall, motor vehicle accident or sports. Additionally, thinning of the bone due to osteoporosis in the elderly can make the bone more susceptible to breaking.

Joint Pain


Joint pain can affect one or more joints. Joint pain can be caused by many types of injuries or conditions. It may be linked to arthritis, bursitis, and muscle pain. No matter what causes it, joint pain can be very bothersome. Joint Pain happens in Hip, Knees, Shoulder, Elbow, Hand , Wrist, Foot & Ankle.

Pediatric Orthopedics


We treat pediatric orthopedic problems, both surgical and nonsurgical. These include developmental disorders of the hip and foot, scoliosis, correction of angular limb deformities and limb lengthening, and care of the pediatric and adolescent trauma patient.

Diagnostic Services
